Why Blood Donation Matters

Life-Saving Impact

One blood donation can save up to three lives. Blood is needed every two seconds, and your donation can make a critical difference for patients in emergency situations, surgeries, and cancer treatments.

Constant Need

Blood has a limited shelf life - red blood cells last only 42 days, and platelets just 5 days. This means there's always a need for fresh donations to maintain adequate blood supplies.

Community Support

Blood donation creates a network of support within communities. When you donate, you're helping neighbors, friends, and family members who may need blood in the future.

Donation Process

1

Registration

Complete a brief registration form with your personal information and medical history.

2

Screening

Undergo a quick health screening including blood pressure, temperature, and hemoglobin check.

3

Donation

The actual donation takes about 8-10 minutes. You'll be comfortable and well-cared for throughout.

4

Recovery

Rest for 10-15 minutes while enjoying refreshments to help your body recover.

Eligibility Requirements

You Can Donate If:

  • You are 18-65 years old
  • You weigh at least 110 pounds
  • You are in good health
  • You haven't donated in the last 56 days
  • You have no recent tattoos or piercings
  • You are not pregnant or nursing

You Cannot Donate If:

  • You have certain medical conditions
  • You are taking certain medications
  • You have traveled to high-risk areas
  • You have had recent surgery
  • You have certain infections
  • You have used intravenous drugs
