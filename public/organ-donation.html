Why Organ Donation Matters

Life-Saving Impact

One organ donor can save up to 8 lives through organ donation and enhance the lives of up to 75 people through tissue donation. Your decision to donate can give someone a second chance at life.

Critical Need

Every 10 minutes, someone is added to the national transplant waiting list. Currently, over 100,000 people are waiting for life-saving organ transplants, and 17 people die each day waiting.

Safe & Regulated

Organ donation is a highly regulated medical process. All potential donors are thoroughly evaluated, and the process follows strict medical and ethical guidelines to ensure safety and respect.

Donation Process

1

Registration

Register as an organ donor through your state's donor registry or when getting your driver's license.

2

Medical Evaluation

When donation becomes possible, medical professionals evaluate your organs for suitability.

3

Matching

Organs are matched with recipients based on blood type, size, medical urgency, and location.

4

Transplantation

Organs are transplanted to recipients, giving them a new chance at life.

Common Myths & Facts

Myth: Doctors won't try to save my life if I'm a donor

Fact: Medical professionals are legally and ethically bound to save your life first. Organ donation is only considered after all efforts to save your life have failed and brain death has been declared.

Myth: I'm too old to donate

Fact: There's no age limit for organ donation. The decision is based on the health and condition of your organs, not your age. Many donors are in their 70s and 80s.

Myth: My family will have to pay for the donation

Fact: There is no cost to the donor's family for organ donation. All costs related to donation are covered by the transplant recipient's insurance or the transplant center.

Myth: I can't have an open-casket funeral

Fact: Organ donation doesn't interfere with traditional funeral arrangements, including open-casket services. The body is treated with respect and dignity throughout the process.
